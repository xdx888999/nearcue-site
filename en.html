03 / Setup

Three steps.
You're ready.

  1. 01

    Install both apps

    Get the iPhone app from the App Store and download the free Mac receiver from this page.

  2. 02

    Enter the pairing code

    With both devices on the same nearby network, enter the six-digit code and approve the iPhone on your Mac.

  3. 03

    Approve your target app

    Grant the Mac accessibility permission, then add the frontmost app you want to control.

About Accessibility permission

NearCue uses it only to send the keys you actively press to the currently approved app. It does not read screen content or control your Mac when you are not interacting with it.

Why can't my iPhone find my Mac?＋

Make sure both devices are on the same Wi-Fi or nearby peer-to-peer network. On iPhone, allow NearCue under Settings → Privacy & Security → Local Network, and keep NearCue running on the Mac.

Why isn't the Mac app in the Mac App Store?＋

NearCue needs Accessibility permission to send the keys you actively trigger to an approved app. The Mac app is distributed from this website, but remains Developer ID signed and notarized by Apple.

Why doesn't my Mac respond?＋

Check that Accessibility permission is granted, the target app is frontmost, and that app has been explicitly approved in NearCue.

Does voice input submit automatically?＋

No. NearCue only inserts recognized text into the current Mac input. You always choose whether to confirm or send it.

How do I restore my one-time purchase?＋

In NearCue settings or the purchase screen on iPhone, tap Restore Purchases and use the Apple Account that made the purchase. The Mac receiver is free and does not need to be purchased again.
